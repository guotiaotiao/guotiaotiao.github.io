Neuroplasticity

Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset

I myself came across this concept many years after becoming an adult. Thinking about the experiences growing up, and the thinking patterns of the people around, it’s really the fixed mindset that dominates. As if only "innate" abilities could be affirmed. So everyone tries to make things easy, or is always trying ways to make a blockbuster without effort, everything is in pursuit of quick success：like how to lose 20kg in a week, how to master a language in 3 days. . . Because if you work hard for years achieve results which someone else achieved easily , you will only become a person who is not smart but only works hard, but smart is the quality we pursuit. Fundamentally, it is the innate divine power that is admired. So everyone wants to be a "genius". What if you find out that you are not a genius? It will be a nightmare. After failure, I can't cheer up. I believe that no matter how hard I work, , I can't succeed at all because I'm not a genius. Life is stagnant, there is no hope anymore.

Is that the truth?

It was a blessing to learn about Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset. No matter what stage of life you are in, you can ignite the flame of hope, and there should be no more moments of despair and disappointment with yourself. Because as long as you keep trying, find a method that suits you, and keep working hard, you will definitely become a better self.

In the recent learning process of foundation, when I encounter difficulties, the fixed mindset will jump out from time to time. For example, when I came into contact with some brand-new concepts for the first time, and temporarily couldn't connect them with the existing knowledge system and cognitive framework, those new knowledge seemed to be floating in the air, but I couldn't catch them. At this point, I would be very frustrated and start to wonder if I am too stupid, if I have no ability to learn coding at all, if all this is a wrong decision.In fact, these difficulties will always pass, and when I start to understand them, what I thought I could not do, I can do.

In this process of going from impossible to being able, a few things are helpful, when exposed to new concepts, new knowledge, unable to understand and master, go to google different resources written by different people. Different examples explaining the same concept and knowledge is easier to understand than a single dimension, because everyone's thinking logic is various and their expressions are different. For example, let's say I see an orange, and when I start looking at it from the side and top, it's actually half an orange, and if I don't change the angle, I always think it's a whole orange . Explaining the same concept from different angles and sources is like looking at the same orange from different angles.
